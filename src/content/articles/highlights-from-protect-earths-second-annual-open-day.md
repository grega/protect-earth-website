## Celebrating Community and Nature: Protect Earth's Second Annual Open Day at High Wood

On a sunny 8th of June, Protect Earth hosted its second annual Open Day at High Wood. Running from 11 am to 4 pm, our Open Day brought together enthusiastic attendees, all eager to learn, engage, and celebrate the ongoing transformation of High Wood from a commercial timber plantation back to a flourishing native broadleaf woodland.

The day blended educational activities, hands-on workshops, and opportunities to discuss and learn. The weather was better than expected, providing the right atmosphere for everyone. Members of Protect Earth, including Kathy, Phil, Steve, Duncan, Ian, and Shane, were on hand to guide activities and share. Shane's son-in-law, a talented chef, treated everyone to a delicious BBQ, adding to the festive mood.

## Engaging Activities for All

The Open Day offered a variety of activities that catered to all ages and interests.

Steve the Ecologist led an enlightening Ecology Walk, during which participants learned about the efforts to restore High Wood and spotted signs of [ancient woodland](https://www.protect.earth/blog/ancient-woodlands-places-of-huge-ecological-diversity) and Cornwall's industrial heritage. Birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts were thrilled to encounter the diverse wildlife inhabiting the woodland during the mid-summer season.

Adding to the historical perspective, author and historian Iain Rowe from Caradon Archaeology captivated attendees with his guided walk, "History Beneath Your Feet." This popular session attracted over 40 participants, eager to uncover the rich history embedded in the landscape of High Wood.

The Screech Owl Sanctuary and Animal Park drew significant attention with their interactive talk and demonstrations, featuring a tawny owl, barn owl, polecat, and two baby egrets. Everyone loved the close-up encounters.

Children also enjoyed crafting bird boxes with Duncan and Steve and starting a bug hotel, fostering a hands-on appreciation for wildlife conservation. Other children's craft activities included making posters and bookmarks. Other activities included a tombola and a scavenger hunt.

Pete from Pole-lathe Turners and Green Woodworkers demonstrated traditional woodworking techniques, inspiring attendees with the beauty of working with natural materials.

Lastly, the Cornwall Wildlife Trust talked informally about their conservation efforts, emphasising the importance of preserving natural habitats throughout Cornwall, of which High Wood is only one site contributing to a network and number of natural habitats for wildlife to share and thrive across the county.

## Community Feedback

The event garnered positive feedback from attendees, reflecting the feedback we get on social media when posting about regular activities to local Facebook groups. It's fantastic that in the short period of time Protect Earth has [taken on High Wood](https://www.protect.earth/blog/high-wood), our goal of restoring the space into its natural state as a native broadleaf woodland with greater access for all has been so readily embraced by private citizens who regularly use High Wood, [local colleges](https://www.protect.earth/blog/from-classroom-to-countryside-oakwood-specialist-college-students-and-protect-earth-unite-for-hedgerow-planting), and the town council.

Kathy, who coordinates the [monthly work parties at High Wood](http://www.protect.earth/high-wood), spoke directly and in person with some of the regular work party volunteers who also attended the day (to point out their hard work and contributions to family and friends). Kev, a qualified forestry professional, has taken a leadership role in guiding larger projects, such as creating ponds and dams at the work parties in the coming months.

One significant area of feedback was the need for improved disability access. Attendees appreciated the efforts so far and encouraged further enhancements to ensure that High Wood is accessible to everyone.
