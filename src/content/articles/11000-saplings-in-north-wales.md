Protect Earth has completed planting 11,000 saplings in Nannerch, North Wales—one of our most significant projects. Combined with our 12,000-tree planting at Goytre, we've stepped up our efforts to restore habitats and boost biodiversity across the UK this season.

To put this into perspective, we celebrated planting 100,000 saplings over four years last year. This single project in Nannerch accounts for more than 10% of that total—on land we are managing for the long term.

## The Landscape

![$2](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/66f54aab97a2451c86736b2d/55a6526d-0a22-41c6-a501-81d01c7d368b/PXL_20250119_140911884.jpg?format=original)

This site sits within the Clwydian Range and Dee Valley Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty, a landscape shaped by farming but rich in ecological potential. The land has been semi-improved grassland, heavily grazed by livestock for years. Some areas, classified as Lowland Dry Acid Grassland, may not look botanically rich but support fascinating grassland fungi. Fragments of ancient woodland—oak, ash, wild cherry, and hazel—add to the site's biodiversity alongside patches of scrub and gorse.

With this in mind, we approached Nannerch with the intention of enhancing biodiversity while working with the land's existing character.

## The Plan to Create a Thriving Natural Habitat

After carefully assessing the site, Steve the Ecologist developed a plan to restore and expand woodland across 19 acres of the 27-acre site. The goal is to create a resilient, species-rich habitat supporting wildlife and carbon sequestration. Protect Earth's plan is to form the new woodland around mainly Oak trees with hazel, birch, aspen, and wild cherry to add variety. The woodland's periphery will be rowan, hawthorn, and crab apple, supporting birds and pollinators.

The Lowland Dry Acid Grassland areas will remain unplanted to protect their unique ecological role.

This species and habitat mix will enable the site to offer homes and food to wildlife quickly, strengthening biodiversity, which will only become richer and richer as these spaces mature.

## Increasing Nannerch's Woodlands

Because this land is within an Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty - and is a protected landscape - our plan underwent rigorous planning and permissions before any work began. Protect Earth took on the role of land manager in 2023, and by early 2025, we were keen, with project approval, to start this very ambitious project.

With 11,000 saplings to plant, efficiency is key. Over two weeks, a professional tree-planting team completed most of the work.

We also invited volunteers—including local council members—to get involved in creating this new woodland.

Community involvement is at the heart of what we do. Before each project, we connect with local people, sharing our plans and inviting their input. During planting, we encourage hands-on participation. Access to green spaces isn't just about conservation—it's about wellbeing, education, and fostering a lasting connection with nature. "We really enjoyed our days tree planting," relayed some tired and satisfied volunteers at the end of a day.

## Looking Ahead: A Future Rich in Wildlife

Over time, this landscape will transform into a rich mosaic of habitats—woodland, scrub, open grassland, and gorse—supporting a wide variety of wildlife while providing a green space for the local community to enjoy.

The 11,000 trees planted here are expected to sequester 3,763 tonnes of carbon over 50 years—a significant step towards restoring the natural world and biodiversity in this part of North Wales.
