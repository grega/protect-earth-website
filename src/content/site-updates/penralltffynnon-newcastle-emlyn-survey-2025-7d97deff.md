A lot of thick, tall ferns were present across the site. Many of the guards, particularly hedge guards, were covered by ferns, with little light penetrating through. A high level of loss was recorded, with approximately 75% dead.

Project manager Mike noted: “…spent a day bracken bashing to find trees so we will go back and replant over winter, and we need to get there with a work party in spring to ensure bracken does not come back with such ferocity. When the fern fronds are small, simply bruising them means they put more vigour into a new stem, and eventually this rolling of bracken removes it. Also, by the time the trees have surpassed 5 to 10 years in growth, it will shade out the bracken and produce a wonderful understory for wildlife and grasses, etc.”
