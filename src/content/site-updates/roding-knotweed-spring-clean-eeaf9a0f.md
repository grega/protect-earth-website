A group of local volunteers removed the canes, rubbish and adjacent scrub from the mega knotweed patch on the Roding. Then the work of clearing the old knotweed stems began. These are dried out and woody in winter, and make dene networks that can be hard to navigate when the new growth comes through. By knocking down and burning all the old stem, and removing rubbish, future treatment will be a lot quicker, safer, and more effective.
