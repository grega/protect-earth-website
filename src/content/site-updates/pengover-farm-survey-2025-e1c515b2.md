One of our main volunteers at Highwood surveyed Pengover Farm.  ‘With a success rate of 70%’, the site is doing really well. 
He reported: “There is a mixture of different trees… that are doing, willow, birch, holly, hawthorn, oak and hazel…. The trees planted along the border with the neighbour’s land have a very high success rate, probably as they are protected by bramble bushes, and the protectors are ready to be removed. No additional planting is necessary.”



There are lots of farmland birds, including yellowhammer, and migrant warblers like chiffchaff and blackcap were noticed on-site in summer. The new woodland extends an area of existing trees and includes areas of both wetter woodland near a stream and drier areas. The woodland is surrounded by meadows which are full of summer butterflies. This mosaic of habitats will benefit a wide range of wildlife.
