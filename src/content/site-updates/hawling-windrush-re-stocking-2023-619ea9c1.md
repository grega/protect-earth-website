March 2023 we came back to Hawling again, to replace 400 failed saplings. This was one of our larger losses, with almost 100% of the previous January planting failing. There were several reasons for this, partly the incredibly hot summer and accompanying drought that hit in Summer 2022, and partly the total failure of most of the cardboard guards we were trialling here. They fell apart in the first few months and many were then eaten by deer. Another part of the problem was the south facing quick draining soil. We should not have planted there, but that was the last project we did before bringing on Steve the Ecologist and we’ve had no similar problems since.
